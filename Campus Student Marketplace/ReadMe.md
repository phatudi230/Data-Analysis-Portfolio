## 📌 Project Overview

An independent Business Analysis case study identifying and 
solving a real operational problem in student-led commerce 
at TUT Soshanguve Campus. Students running informal 
businesses relied entirely on fragmented WhatsApp groups 
for marketing, resulting in message fatigue, zero stock 
visibility and a high-friction manual payment process.

This project followed the full BA lifecycle — from problem 
identification and stakeholder research to requirements 
documentation, process mapping and solution design.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Campus commerce at TUT Soshanguve was plagued by:
- Constant repetitive WhatsApp posting causing message 
fatigue among 200+ group members
- Businesses only visible within specific residence sections 
— not across the wider campus
- No real-time stock or service availability information
- A high-friction 8-step manual purchase process with an 
"Awkward Waiting Moment" during every EFT payment
- Service providers cancelling appointments due to academic 
pressure with no structured scheduling tool

**Key question:** How can student-led campus businesses 
operate more efficiently while respecting academic 
commitments?

## 👥 Stakeholder Research

### Supply Side — Student Sellers
Conducted interview with a Nail Technician identifying:
- Posting works as marketing but creates notification fatigue
- Academic pressure causes appointment cancellations 
damaging customer trust
- Desire for campus-wide visibility beyond one section
- Need for daily appointment limits to protect study time

### Demand Side — Student Buyers
Conducted ethnographic research by acting as a student 
buyer, mapping the complete purchase journey:

| Step | Action | Friction Point |
|------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Search for item manually | No centralized directory |
| 2 | Send DM to seller | Manual effort, high friction |
| 3 | Ask about availability | No real-time stock view |
| 4 | Confirm payment method | Repetitive communication |
| 5 | Send order and room number | Privacy concern |
| 6 | Wait for delivery | Manual coordination |
| 7 | Add seller as beneficiary | CRITICAL — Awkward Waiting Moment |
| 8 | Perform EFT and show proof | Trust issue |

### Non-Functional Requirements
| ID | Requirement |
|----|-------------|
| NFR1 | Data-lite performance for limited data bundles |
| NFR2 | Encrypted student data and room numbers |
| NFR3 | Mobile-first interface |
| NFR4 | 24/7 platform availability |

## 💡 Proposed Solution — Student Online Mall (SOM)

A centralized mobile marketplace combining social media 
engagement with structured commerce functionality.

**Customer Side:**
- Social feed with seller posts, likes, comments and follows
- Mall section with categorized products and services
- Real-time stock visibility per product
- Cash on delivery or online transfer payment selection
- Service booking with weekly schedule view

**Seller Side:**
- Product and service posting
- Open/Closed store toggle
- Daily appointment limit control
- Stock management and updates
- Order and appointment notifications
- Payment receipts

**TO-BE Process Improvement:**

| Step | Improvement |
|------|-------------|
| Browse inventory | Eliminates availability PM |
| Add to cart | Order documented automatically |
| Select payment | No back-and-forth needed |
| Pay via QR code | Removes manual beneficiary setup |
| Seller notified instantly | No manual coordination |
| QR scan confirms receipt | Eliminates Awkward Waiting Moment |
