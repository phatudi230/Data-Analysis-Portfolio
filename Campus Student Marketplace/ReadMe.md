Lead Business Analyst (Independent Case Study)

Campus commerce at TUT Soshanguve was plagued by "Information Asymmetry" and high-friction manual processes. Students relied on fragmented WhatsApp groups, resulting in message fatigue, lack of stock visibility, and inefficient payment workflows.
I designed a centralized commerce solution aimed at professionalizing student-led businesses and enhancing the buyer experience. My contributions included:

Process Mapping & Friction Analysis: Conducted interviews and "shadowing" to map the end-to-end user journey, identifying that manual payment setup was the primary cause of transaction drop-offs.

Strategic Solution Design: Conceptualized the "Student Online Mall," featuring real-time stock tracking and automated appointment scheduling to help sellers balance academic duties with business growth.

Validation & Impact Modeling: Established clear success metrics, including a projected 60% reduction in checkout time through QR-code integration and a "Market Transparency" framework that allows buyers to instantly find alternatives, creating a more competitive and accessible campus economy.
Core Competencies Applied:

Elicitation Techniques: Used Prototype-Led Elicitation to bridge the gap between user needs and technical functionality.

Systems Thinking: Designed features (like the "Appointment Limit") that address both technical efficiency and the mental well-being of student entrepreneurs.

Stakeholder Requirements: Translated informal student complaints into structured business requirements.
