# Institutional Policy Gap Analysis
### Universal Residence Storage Policy — TUT Soshanguve Campus

---

##  Project Overview

An independent policy analysis and advocacy initiative 
addressing the arbitrary withdrawal of storage services 
from Sosh5 Residence at TUT Soshanguve Campus. This project 
demonstrates real-world application of gap analysis, 
compliance research, business writing and stakeholder 
engagement — resulting in a measurable institutional outcome.

---

## The Problem

TUT residence administration withdrew storage services from 
Sosh5 Residence during the June recess period without 
formal policy justification or adequate prior notice. This 
created an unreasonable burden on students — particularly 
those from other provinces who could not transport large 
belongings such as fridges across the country for a short 
recess period.

**The core issue:** Storage was being withdrawn based on 
administrative discretion despite no policy clause 
prohibiting it — and despite the institution's own rules 
explicitly acknowledging storage arrangements as permitted.

---

##  Tools & Methods

- Policy Document Analysis
- Compliance Research & Gap Analysis
- Formal Proposal Writing
- Stakeholder Engagement & Advocacy
- Governance Structure Navigation

---

##  Gap Analysis Findings

A deep-dive audit of TUT Residence Rules and Administrative 
Processes revealed:

**Rule 8.7 / Rule 5.13.6:**
*"Students who make arrangements to leave their belongings 
in storage do so at their own risk."*

This clause explicitly acknowledges and permits storage 
arrangements — directly contradicting the administration's 
position. The institution had a legal and policy basis for 
storage that was being ignored by administrative practice.

**Rule 8.6 / Rule 5.13.5:**
*"If there is no operational need to utilise the facility 
during June/July holidays, alternative arrangements will 
be communicated."*

This placed a formal obligation on management to communicate 
alternative arrangements — an obligation that was not met.

---

## Proposal Strategy

The formal proposal was structured around three arguments:

| Argument | Basis |
|----------|-------|
| Storage is policy-permitted | Rule 8.7 / 5.13.6 |
| Management has communication obligation | Rule 8.6 / 5.13.5 |
| Withdrawal creates unreasonable burden | Rule 4.1 — duty of care |

**Interim measure strategy:** Rather than only requesting 
a permanent policy change, the proposal included an interim 
measure request for the immediate recess period — lowering 
the risk threshold for management to say yes and creating 
a proof of concept for the long-term policy.

---

##  Stakeholder Engagement

Navigated a multi-level governance structure independently:
Residence Committee → ARSC → CRC → IRC → RGC
- Approached Residence Committee to arrange meeting 
— no action taken
- Submitted formal proposal directly to SRC SSS officer
- Followed up through proper escalation channels
- Engaged HOD Accommodation when SSS was unresponsive

---

##  Outcome

**Storage was successfully reinstated for Sosh5 Residence 
students for the June recess period following formal 
submission of the proposal.**

This demonstrates that a well-researched, evidence-based 
proposal submitted through proper governance channels 
can achieve real institutional change — even when initiated 
by a single student independently.
